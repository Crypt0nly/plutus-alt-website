Questions, answered.

Is it really free to start?

Yes. The free plan is the full operating system with a monthly allowance of work — enough to pilot Plutus on real tasks. No credit card, no trial clock. When it proves itself, upgrading takes one click.

Do I need to install anything?

No — Plutus runs in your browser, and you can message it from WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack or Discord too. Want it to work on your own computer as well? A small companion app connects your machine in about a minute.

Will it do things without asking?

Only where you've allowed it. Out of the box, anything important waits for sign-off. Put a job on autopilot, though, and Plutus wakes on a heartbeat and runs the whole loop by himself — every step on the record. You can widen or narrow what runs alone at any time, quiet hours included.

What does it work with?

Gmail, Google Calendar, Google Drive, Notion, GitHub, WhatsApp, Telegram, Slack and Discord — plus the web itself, and even your own computer if you connect it. You add accounts one by one, and you can disconnect any of them at any time.

Is our data private?

Your company's workspace is sealed off from every other customer's. Plutus can only reach the accounts you've chosen to connect, and you can take any of them away in one click.

Can the whole company use it?

Yes — that's the point. Everyone delegates to one shared Plutus with shared memory and knowledge, admins decide who may do what, and there's one place to see everything that got done.

How long does rollout take?

Minutes, not months. Plutus runs in the browser, connects to your tools one sign-in at a time, and needs no IT project. Most teams hand it real work on day one.
